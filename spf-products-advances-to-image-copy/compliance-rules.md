# Compliance Rules — Amazon US Dietary Supplements

## The Core Rule (2026.3.31 onward)

**Listing content is now a compliance document.** Amazon will delist products whose listing content does not match the Supplement Facts Panel (SFP) on the packaging.

## Absolute Prohibitions

### Ingredient Expression
- Ingredient names, amounts, units, and forms must be **character-by-character identical** to SFP
- No "Raw Herb Equivalent" conversions
- No extraction ratios (10:1, 20:1, 50:1)
- No "hidden information" not printed on the label
- No approximate or equivalent substitutions

### Health Claims
- No disease treatment, prevention, diagnosis, or cure claims (FDA drug claims)
- No implied therapeutic effects
- No efficacy comparison claims ("stronger", "more potent", "superior absorption")
- No data or claims unsupported by the product label
- No marketing language implying functions the product doesn't have

## What IS Allowed

### Structure/Function Claims (safe zone)
- "Supports [body structure/function]"
- "Promotes [healthy process]"
- "Helps maintain [normal function]"
- "Contributes to [wellness area]"
- "Aids in [general support]"

### Factual Statements
- Ingredient amounts matching SFP
- GRAS certification status
- Third-party testing (SGS, NSF, etc.)
- Patent counts and types
- Manufacturing process (cGMP)
- Serving size and directions from label
- "Free from" claims (if backed by testing)

### Educational Content
- General health topic education (with disclaimer "For educational purposes only")
- Published research citations (must use structure/function language in summary)
- Mechanism of action explanations (educational, not therapeutic)

## Required Disclaimers

Place on any image containing structure/function claims:
```
*These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration.
This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.
```

## Conversion Examples

| Risky (non-compliant) | Safe (compliant) |
|---|---|
| "Reduces wrinkles by 40%" | "Supports skin smoothness and firmness*" |
| "Increases collagen by 28%" | "Promotes natural collagen production*" |
| "Anti-aging formula" | "Supports healthy aging" |
| "Repairs damaged cells" | "Helps protect cells from oxidative stress" |
| "Treats mitochondrial dysfunction" | "Supports mitochondrial health and cellular energy" |
| "Restores ovarian function" | "Supports women's reproductive wellness*" |
| "10x more absorbable" | "Featuring [exact SFP ingredient form]" |
| "Clinically proven to reduce..." | "In a study, participants reported improvements in...*" |
| "The strongest antioxidant" | "A potent antioxidant that helps protect cells" |
| "Eliminates free radicals" | "Helps neutralize free radicals" |

## Metaphor Compliance

| Metaphor direction | Allowed? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Energy, vitality, support, maintain | Yes | Within structure/function scope |
| Rejuvenation, restoration, reversal | No | Implies treatment |
| Shield, protection, defense | Yes | If antioxidant-related |
| Repair, cure, fix | No | Drug claims |
| Wellness, balance, harmony | Yes | General wellness |
| Before/after transformation | No | Implies treatment effect |

## Risk Triggers for Amazon Review

These patterns flag listings for manual review:
- Efficacy comparison (better/stronger/faster than competitors)
- Absorption rate claims
- Clinical outcome percentages without proper context
- Ingredient descriptions diverging from SFP
- Data citations not matching label claims
