# Persuasion Framework — Competitor Reference (FineNutri)

Extracted from FineNutri (斐萃) "Women's Small Purple Bottle" product on JD Health. This is the structural reference for how top-performing supplement brands build persuasion sequences. Adapted for Amazon US compliance.

## Original Persuasion Sequence (18 screens)

FineNutri uses this exact flow to convert browsers to buyers:

1. **Packaging upgrade** → signals product evolution, implies popularity
2. **Hero shot + data bomb** → product image + 4 precise metrics (-40.17% wrinkles, +28.47% collagen)
3. **Certifications** → SGS, 15 patents, toxicity testing
4. **Clinical results (face)** → D0→D28→D56→D84 timeline with before/after photos
5. **Clinical results (body)** → Face/neck/arm/leg grid, each with a percentage
6. **Problem definition** → Wrinkles/sagging/jawline loss + Harvard/Cell journal citations
7. **Mechanism explanation** → Dual mechanism: mitochondrial energy + "garden" care
8. **Ingredient breakdown** → 4 core ingredients, each with a "role label"
9. **Academic publication** → WILEY journal screenshot + plain-language annotation
10. **Mechanism metaphor** → Wilted flower vs glowing flower (before/after)
11. **Deep data** → Bar charts (FSH, cycle, protein expression)
12. **Experiment results + awards** → +60% mitochondria chart + 7 industry trophies
13. **Safety testing** → 5-category icon matrix + 3 user scenarios
14. **Institutional partnerships** → Peking University lab, Jinan University center
15. **Brand institute** → FineNutri Research Institute mission
16. **Product specs** → Full ingredient table + product photo
17. **FAQ** → 6 common questions

## Persuasion Principles (Extracted, Brand-Agnostic)

These are the transferable principles from FineNutri's approach:

### Principle 1: Data Bomb First
Lead with quantified information, not emotional appeals. FineNutri's hero image hits with 4 precise numbers immediately. Adaptation for Amazon: use ingredient amounts + certification counts + serving specs as your "data bomb."

### Principle 2: Problem Before Solution
Screen 6 (problem definition) comes BEFORE screen 7 (mechanism). Make the consumer aware of the issue first, then present how ingredients address it. Adaptation: use general health education ("Oxidative stress affects cellular health as we age") before introducing ingredients.

### Principle 3: Every Ingredient Has a Role
FineNutri gives each ingredient a "character label." Adaptation for compliance: use scientific source labels instead of marketing nicknames.
- Original: "[5th-gen free radical killer] Ergothioneine"
- Compliant: "[GRAS-Certified Antioxidant] Ergothioneine 60mg"

### Principle 4: Translate Science to One Sentence
FineNutri shows a WILEY paper screenshot with a one-sentence Chinese annotation. Adaptation: show English paper title + one plain-English structure/function summary.

### Principle 5: Safety as Icon Matrix
FineNutri uses 5 circular icons for safety tests. The visual density of icons creates "comprehensive testing" perception. Adaptation: "Free From" icon matrix (Hormone-Free, Pesticide-Free, etc.)

### Principle 6: Timeline Creates Expectation
FineNutri's D0→D28→D56→D84 timeline makes results feel predictable. Adaptation: "Week 4 / Week 8 / Week 12" milestones with structure/function language + "individual results may vary."

### Principle 7: Trust Hierarchy (Strong → Weak)
FineNutri orders trust signals by weight:
1. Clinical data (strongest)
2. Published research
3. Institutional partnerships
4. Certifications & patents
5. Industry awards
6. Safety testing
7. Industry associations

Adaptation for Amazon US: reorder to prioritize what Amazon/Alexa values most:
1. SFP consistency (Alexa priority)
2. Third-party testing (safety filter)
3. Published research (AI overview)
4. Patents (differentiation)
5. Awards (visual trust, low Alexa weight)

## What NOT to Copy from FineNutri

These FineNutri tactics are effective on JD Health but non-compliant on Amazon US:

- Precise outcome percentages ("-40.17% wrinkles") → disease treatment claims
- "Garden" metaphor for ovaries → implies treating reproductive conditions
- Wilted vs glowing flower visuals → before/after treatment implication
- Chinese university partnerships → low trust signal for US consumers
- Promotional urgency tactics ("618 sale") → not applicable to evergreen Amazon listings
- Packaging upgrade announcements → irrelevant for new Amazon listings
