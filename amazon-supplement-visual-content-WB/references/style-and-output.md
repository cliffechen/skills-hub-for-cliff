# Style and Output Reference

## Voice Target

Use a clinical minimalist voice inspired by The Ordinary and Timeline:

- ingredient-led
- precise
- restrained
- calm
- scientific without sounding like a medical device or drug
- transparent about what is known and what is not known
- low-hype, low-adjective

Never copy brand slogans, distinctive phrases, product names, trade dress, or brand claims. Do not imply partnership or endorsement.

## Copy Style

Prefer:

- short headlines
- one evidence anchor per module
- exact label terms
- plain body-function language
- quiet confidence
- clean structure

Avoid:

- "miracle," "breakthrough," "revolutionary," "ultimate," "best," "instant"
- emotional pressure
- pseudo-science
- disease language
- inflated longevity language
- vague "wellness" filler with no product fact

## Sequence-Level Information Design

Before writing final image copy, map the whole image set as one A+ story. Each image should have a distinct job.

Common roles:

| Role | Best use | Avoid repeating |
|---|---|---|
| Hero hook | Reframe the buyer's belief or introduce the ingredient idea | Full Supplement Facts details |
| Ingredient story | Explain what the ingredient is and why it matters | Dose, serving size, and serving count if already covered |
| Mechanism education | Show a conservative pathway or support area | Disease language, repair/reverse/regenerate claims |
| Routine fit | Show how the product fits daily use | Over-explaining ingredient science |
| Formula facts | Show exact label facts, dose, count, form, and other ingredients | Brand manifesto copy |
| Brand or packaging philosophy | Explain clarity, minimalism, label transparency, or packaging if proven | Unsupported sustainability/certification claims |
| Proof or certification | Show substantiated badges, facility facts, COA, or testing claims | Claims without uploaded proof |
| FAQ/comparison | Answer objections and reduce confusion | Repeating the hero slogan |

Anti-repetition rule:

- Use each major fact deliberately. If `1,000 mg per serving`, `2 capsules`, or `45 servings` already appears in one image, do not repeat it in another image unless that second layout is specifically a formula-facts or comparison module.
- Do not repeat the same support triad in every image. Rotate the angle: one image can say cellular/mitochondrial/energy support, another can tell the Urolithin A story, and another can explain routine fit or label transparency.
- Check repeated meaning, not only repeated wording. "Start with the cell" and "cellular support begins below the surface" may be different words but still the same idea if they answer the same buyer question.
- If the user notices repetition, treat that as a signal to revise the sequence map, not only the sentence.
- A split layout should not make both sides do the same job. Let one side carry story or positioning and the other side carry product facts.
- If evidence or brand context is not enough to create a fresh angle, ask the user for the missing direction. Do not fill the empty module with repeated label facts or generic wellness language.

Common non-repeating substitutions:

| If already covered | Do not repeat with new wording | Use a different role instead |
|---|---|---|
| Dose/serving facts | "clear serving", "label-led dose", "daily amount" | Brand philosophy, packaging design, proof, FAQ |
| Cellular support story | "start with the cell", "below the surface", "cell-first support" | Purity/testing, routine fit, product form, packaging |
| No proprietary blend | "nothing hidden", "dose not buried", "transparent formula" | Design philosophy, ingredient origin, third-party report |
| Support triad | cellular/mitochondrial/energy repeated together | One specific buyer question or one substantiated proof point |
| Clinical-minimal brand idea | "quiet formula", "less noise", "clear label" repeated | Visual packaging intent, typography/layout, carton-to-capsule experience |

## Image Copy Length Guide

For Amazon image text:

- Main headline: 2 to 7 words
- Subhead: 6 to 16 words
- Callout: 2 to 6 words
- Footnote: only when the layout clearly supports it

For A+ modules:

- Module headline: 3 to 9 words
- Body: 1 to 3 short sentences
- Callouts: 2 to 4 items

## Final Delivery Tone

Explain to the user in Chinese. Keep customer-facing Amazon copy in English unless the user requests Chinese copy.

For Markdown deliverables:

- Section explanations must be in Chinese.
- Layout notes, usage recommendations, risk notes, and next actions must be in Chinese.
- Customer-facing image text, A+ module text, and alt text should stay in English by default.
- Avoid dry compliance-only drafts when the layout is a hero or brand-opening image. Provide several creative directions with different risk levels, then mark which version is recommended.
