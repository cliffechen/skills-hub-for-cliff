# Compliance Reference

## Source Priority

Use evidence in this order:

1. Actual product label and Supplement Facts panel supplied by the user.
2. User-provided substantiation, COA, clinical study, or brand-approved claim matrix.
3. Official FDA, eCFR, Amazon Seller Central, and About Amazon sources.
4. Internal project reference files supplied by the user.

If sources conflict, choose the safer and more literal claim. Do not fill gaps with category assumptions.

## Supplement Facts Exact-Match Rule

For Amazon US dietary supplements, treat the Supplement Facts panel as the copy boundary:

- Ingredient name must match the label.
- Amount, unit, serving size, form, extract wording, source wording, and Daily Value status must match the label.
- Do not convert, round, simplify, or translate ingredient facts in image copy.
- Do not state raw herb equivalents, extract ratio benefits, "high potency," "concentrated," "enhanced absorption," or hidden active information unless the label and substantiation support that exact statement.
- If an ingredient or dose is not on the Supplement Facts panel or adjacent label, do not use it as a selling point.

## FDA Structure/Function Boundaries

Dietary supplement structure/function claims may describe support for normal body structure or function, but they must be truthful, not misleading, substantiated, and reviewed for DSHEA requirements.

Safer patterns:

- "supports healthy [normal function]"
- "helps maintain [normal structure/function]"
- "designed to support [normal wellness area]"
- "with [exact label ingredient] [exact amount] per serving"

Higher-risk patterns:

- "boosts," "repairs," "reverses," "restores," "regenerates," "detoxes," "burns fat," "flushes toxins"
- "clinically proven" without exact study support
- "doctor recommended" without documented basis
- "anti-aging" if it implies disease reversal or measurable age reversal
- "anti-inflammatory" because inflammation is commonly tied to disease/drug claims

Blocked patterns:

- "diagnose," "treat," "cure," "prevent," "heal"
- disease names or symptoms as promised outcomes
- drug-like claims, pain relief, anxiety relief, depression relief, blood pressure control, blood sugar control, cholesterol lowering, cancer support, arthritis relief, UTI cure, IBS treatment, fatty liver support, Alzheimer's prevention
- guaranteed outcomes, before/after promises, time-bound medical results

## DSHEA Disclaimer

When a label or listing uses structure/function claims, FDA rules require the standard dietary supplement disclaimer and notification review. Do not decide legal placement yourself. Flag this for regulatory review:

"These statements have not been evaluated by the Food and Drug Administration. This product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease."

## Amazon Image and A+ Caution

Amazon may reject claims that cure, mitigate, treat, or prevent disease, especially in food, health products, vitamins, and supplements. Keep A+ and image copy consistent with:

- Supplement Facts panel
- product label
- title, bullets, description, backend attributes
- A+ module body text
- alt text and OCR-readable image text
